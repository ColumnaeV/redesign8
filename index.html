About Me

Hello there! My name is Christian and I’m the developer of this site. I’ve recently left Millard North High School whilst graduating with my regular and IB diplomas in pursuit of higher education. Currently, I am attending university in the state of California as a second-year majoring in Biomedical Engineering.

As a freelancing website developer and graphic designer, I spend most of my freed time working on digital portfolios and creating new pieces of art. Within the last couple of years, I have been developing an expertise in graphic design whilst advancing my artistic ability in frontend design. Atypical to my current technological repertoire, I plan on pursuing my humanitarian ideals by providing for those who are in need of immediate care through my future studies in the field of medicine.

Why Biomedical Engineering?

Healing.

This single word is what has been my guiding objective for my pursuit into the field of medicine. In today's world, hate has drawn towards harm, violence, and destruction of both physical and emotional realities. Healing as a random stranger, friend, and brother has always been a core value in which my life has been built upon. After years of wearing and testing, this part of me has always stayed strong, but how does it apply to biomedical engineering?

Studying biomedical engineering will open doors for me to pursue knowledge and experience close to modern medicine and patient care. Innovating prosthetics has always been of great interest regardless of I am able to pursue clinical work as a physican or not. Direct and intensive/emergency care of patients is something in which I see myself doing as an occupation in the future.

Developing C&C Studios: The Beginning

This organisation was founded under the fundamental idea of shared community knowledge. It first started out between my cousin, Camille, and I as a project in which we cound learn from each other: her teaching me photography and me teaching her web development. This project, a photography website for her, was close to fruition, but we were stalled at determining the best domain name to buy for her. Considering the positive learning environment in which this project was constructed around, we started to build this idea of an organisation that promoted artistic exposure and learning, a place in which we could all admire each other's work and draw inspiration from, a studio.

At the time, it was just Camille and I, hence C&C, but as of now we have expanded to six different forms of digital art.
